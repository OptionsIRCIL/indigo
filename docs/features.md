# Features

Indigo provides the following features to Community Navigation Services:

## Consumer Relations

Indigo provides tools for recording consumer contact and demographic
information, setting and measuring individual goals, and tracking direct
services rendered to the consumer.

### Contact and Demographic Information

The following data points can be stored for a given individual:

 - Legal Name
 - Nicknames / AKAs
 - Home Address
 - Phone Number(s)
 - Email
 - Disabilities
 - Race / Ethnicity
 - Gender

### Fuzzy Search and Deduplication

To reduce the number of duplicate records, the search functionality in Indigo 
has both autocomplete and fuzzy finding. This means that inexact searches are 
more likely to give results of similar records.

Additionally, duplicate records for the same individual may be merged.

### User Input

User Input is guided by mechanisms that help ensure (but does not guarantee) the correctness of the input.

- Input fields restricted to only valid options
- Alerts for quantities that seem too large or small

### Data Retention and Disposal

HIPAA and Options IRCIL policy dictate that records shall be retained for at 
least seven years.

Once a record is closed, and seven years have passed since the closure date, 
nearly all individual PII (such as name, location, etc.) can be purged.

It cannot be purged earlier than seven years after the closure date, unless 
the duration is overridden by an administrator.

Anonymized statistical data may be retained if desired, or can be discarded 
alongside the PII.

At this time, disposal will be manually invoked by an administrator.

Organizational records, and other non-consumer related individual records as 
selected by administrators, are exempt from expunction.

### Goal Tracking

To best aid consumers in achieving their independent living goals, tools are
available within Indigo to assist in the creation of Independent Living Plans
as well as a waived plan, if the consumer wishes.

### Direct Service

Where service is rendered to a consumer by a Community Navigation Service,
Indigo has functionality to track what was requested, what outcome was acheived,
any relevant locations visited, and worker time utilization.

## Interaction Logging

To standardize and log call contents, Indigo provides a framework for 
interactions between consumers and Consumer Service Provider to be recorded
for future semi-automated retrival. By utilizing this framework, Community 
Navigation Services can ensure all communications can be indexed for future
reference, and consumers and Consumer Services Providers are provided with 
all possible knowledge and resources available to achieve their independent 
living goals.

## Service Cataloging

Indigo also centralizes cataloging of local programs and services. Following
Inform USA's standards for service cataloging, organizations and agencies, 
sites and locations, and programs and services can be stored, linked, and 
revised with ease. Tools are also provided to remind Consumer Navigation
Services when it's time to contact an organization or agency to review 
any previously discussed programs and services.

## PDF Output

Nearly all data from Indigo may be output from the database into a printable PDF 
format. The structure of the forms and the data contained is specified 
in the Report Builder. Precautions are in place to prevent the misuse of PII 
via the redaction of said information when not strictly neccessary.

## Data Review

Indigo contains tools to assist in the manual review of the data used to 
populate reports by Administrators.

- Birds-Eye Tabular View
- Automatic flagging of quantities that seem too large or small

### Report Builder

The Report Builder is an interface for creation custom PDF reports. 
Reports may include text literals, text input, and tables.
Tables can be populated with almost all data within Indigo, with some 
exceptions for privacy and confidentiality reasons. Each report is given a 
unique name and can be saved for future reuse.
