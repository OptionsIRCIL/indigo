# Terminology

## Abstract

To write software that is both adherent to standards and commutable to CNSes
other than Options IRCIL, Indigo adopts most of its terminology from 
the [ILRU](https://www.ilru.org/uploads/legacy/Alphabet_Soup_Independent_Living_Acronyms_0.pdf) and [IL T&TA](https://ilttacenter.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/NCIL-2025-Independent-Living-Acronyms.pdf), while adherring to the design practices from [Inform USA](https://www.informusa.org/)'s
[CNS Standards v10.1](https://www.informusa.org/standards).

This is an non-exhaustive list of terms used by Indigo and Options IRCIL.

## Acronyms

- ADA - The [Americans with Disabilities Act](https://www.ada.gov/)
- CEO - Community, Education, and Outreach
- CIF - Consumer Information File (f.k.a. Consumer Service Record, CSR)
- CIL - [Center(s) for Independent Living](https://ncil.org/about/independent-living/)
- HIPPA - The [Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act](https://www.cdc.gov/phlp/php/resources/health-insurance-portability-and-accountability-act-of-1996-hipaa.html)
- I&R - Information and Referral (a.k.a Community Navigation)
- ILP - Independent Living Plan
- MFP - Money Follows the Person
- PII - Personally Identifiable Information
- PPR - The [Program Performance Report](https://www.ilru.org/resources/resources-704-reporting) (f.k.a 704 Reporting)
- PWD - Person/People with Disabilities
- VR - Vocational Rehabilitation

### North Dakota Specific

- ADRL - The [Aging and Disability Resource Link](https://ndcarechoice.hhs.nd.gov/)
- TDP - The [Transition and Diversion Program](https://www.nd.gov/eforms/Doc/sfn00649.pdf)

### Minnesota Specific

- SILC - The [Statewide Independent Living Council](https://mn.gov/deed/job-seekers/disabilities/councils/silc.jsp)

## Definitions

- Assistive Technology: Any physical device meant primarily to assist a person with a disabilty. Includes "low tech" as well as "high tech" devices.
- Consumer: Any recipient of Options' services.
- Grant: The source of funding that pays for work done by an employee. Most commonly, this is grant funding through a particular state. 
- Independent Living: The philiosophy of empowering people with disabilities to be able to live as independently as possible.
